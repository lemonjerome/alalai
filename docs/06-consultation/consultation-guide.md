# Consultation Session — User Guide

## What Is the Consultation Room?
The consultation room is where patients and doctors meet virtually for their appointment. It uses Jitsi Meet — a free, browser-based video conferencing platform that requires no installation or account.

---

## How to Join a Consultation

1. In your **Appointments** list, find the appointment you want to join
2. Click **Join** — the button activates **15 minutes before** and **15 minutes after** the scheduled time
3. Outside this window, the button is dimmed and cannot be clicked
4. Clicking Join opens the pre-join screen

---

## Pre-Join Screen

Before entering the room, you see a summary screen showing:
- Both participants' avatars (patient and doctor)
- Scheduled date and time
- Session duration
- A countdown timer if the session hasn't started yet

Click **Join Consultation** to enter the Jitsi Meet room.

---

## Inside the Consultation Room

Jitsi Meet runs in your browser — no download needed. Controls you'll see:
- **Microphone** — mute/unmute yourself
- **Camera** — turn video on/off
- **Screen share** — share your screen (useful for sharing documents or results)
- **Chat** — text chat within the room
- **End call** — leave the room (the other participant remains)

---

## After the Consultation

- Both participants can leave by clicking the End Call button in Jitsi
- Doctors: after the session, navigate to **Consultation Notes** or the appointment detail to add your notes and prescriptions
- Patients: once the doctor adds notes, you'll receive a "Medical Record Available" notification

---

## Technical Requirements

- A modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
- Camera and microphone permissions granted in browser
- Stable internet connection recommended

---

## Joining Window

The Join button is available **±15 minutes** from the scheduled appointment time. This window prevents the room from being open all day while ensuring both parties have enough time to prepare. The room stays accessible for the full duration plus 15 minutes.

---

## What Happens If One Party Doesn't Show?

The room stays open during the join window. If the other party doesn't join, you can leave and contact them via the app's notification system. Doctors can mark the appointment as **No Show** if the patient does not attend.
