# Medical Records — Patient Guide

## Overview
The Medical Records section is your complete consultation history. Every time a doctor creates a record after your consultation, it appears here. You can read the diagnosis, review prescriptions, and print a formal prescription card to take to a pharmacy.

---

## Finding Your Records

In the patient sidebar, click **Medical Records**. You'll see a timeline of all your past consultations, sorted newest first.

Each entry in the timeline shows:
- Consultation date and day of week
- Doctor's name
- Diagnosis (first line)
- Number of prescriptions (if any)
- Follow-up appointment badge (if the doctor scheduled one)

---

## Reading a Record

Click any consultation entry to open the full record. You'll see:

### Diagnosis & Notes
- **Diagnosis** — The doctor's official diagnosis or working diagnosis
- **Consultation Notes** — Detailed notes from the doctor about what was discussed, symptoms observed, and examination findings
- **Follow-Up Recommended** (green banner, if set) — The date the doctor recommends you return for a check-up

### Prescriptions
If the doctor prescribed medication, you'll see a formal prescription card with:
- Doctor's name and the platform name
- Consultation date
- Your name
- A table of all medications with dosage, frequency, duration, and any special instructions
- A signature placeholder

---

## Printing a Prescription

1. Open the record that has prescriptions
2. Click **Print Prescription** in the top-right corner
3. Your browser's print dialog opens — the prescription card is formatted for print (the rest of the page is hidden)
4. Print to paper or save as PDF

> **Tip**: Most pharmacies accept printed prescriptions from telehealth platforms. If a pharmacy requires an original document, contact your doctor through the app.

---

## Pagination

If you have more than 10 records, use the **Previous** and **Next** buttons at the bottom of the Records page to navigate between pages.

---

## Edge Cases

- **No records yet** — If you haven't had any completed consultations with notes, the timeline will show a placeholder message
- **Record not found** — If you click a link and the record doesn't exist, you'll be redirected to the records list
- **No prescription** — If the doctor didn't prescribe anything, the prescription section won't appear; the Print button also won't appear
- **Incorrect record** — If you believe a record contains an error, please contact your doctor. Patients cannot edit medical records.

---

## Privacy

- You can only see your own records — no other patient's records are accessible
- Doctors can see records for appointments they conducted with you
- Records are permanent and cannot be deleted by patients
